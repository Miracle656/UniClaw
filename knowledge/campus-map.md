## Campus Overview
UNILAG occupies approximately **200 hectares** on the Lagos Lagoon waterfront in Akoka, Yaba, Lagos. The main campus is large — walking from one end to the other takes 15–25 minutes. There is also a **second campus at Idi-Araba, Surulere** (College of Medicine + LUTH).

## Getting Around Campus

### On Foot
- Most lecture-to-lecture journeys: 5–15 minutes walking
- Wear comfortable shoes — the campus has a mix of paved paths and unpaved roads
- During rain: Some areas flood — know alternate routes or invest in good sandals

### Okada (Motorcycle Taxis)
- Available at the main gate and key junctions inside campus
- **Within campus:** ₦100 – ₦300 per drop
- Negotiate before boarding — quote a price first
- Faster than walking but use with caution (wear helmet if possible)

## Getting to UNILAG

### From Lagos Island / Victoria Island
- **Danfo (bus):** Take bus to Yaba / CMS direction → connect to Akoka
- **Ride-hailing (Bolt/Uber):** 30–60 min depending on traffic, ₦2,500 – ₦6,000
- **Third Mainland Bridge** → Mainland → Yaba → Akoka

### From Yaba
- 10–20 minutes by danfo or keke
- Danfo from Yaba bus stop → Akoka / UNILAG gate: ₦100 – ₦200

### From Surulere / Ikorodu Road / Other Mainland
- Various danfo routes — ask for "UNILAG gate" or "Akoka"
- Bolt/Uber also viable from most mainland locations

## FAQs

**Q: I'm new — how do I find my faculty building?**
A: Ask at the main gate — security staff know every building. Or ask any student you see. UNILAG students are generally helpful to lost freshers.

**Q: Is it safe to walk around campus at night?**
A: Main campus pathways are generally safe with other students around. Avoid isolated areas at night. Stick to lit paths and go with friends after 9pm.

**Q: Where do I park if I drive to campus?**
A: Staff/student parking available near faculty buildings. Student parking is limited — a campus sticker/permit may be required [VERIFY].

**Q: How far is CMUL from main campus?**
A: About 7–10km by road (Surulere). Takes 30–60 minutes depending on Lagos traffic.

**Q: Where's the nearest ATM?**
A: Several ATMs on campus — near the Student Centre and Admin buildings. Also at Akoka road outside the main gate (Access, GTB, First Bank branches nearby).
