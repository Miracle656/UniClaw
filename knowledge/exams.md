## Academic Calendar Structure

UNILAG runs a **two-semester session**:
- **Session:** September – July (approximately)
- **First Semester:** September – February
- **Second Semester:** February – July

### Typical Semester Breakdown
| Phase | Duration |
|---|---|
| Registration period | Weeks 1–4 |
| Lectures | Weeks 1–12 |
| Revision/Study week | Week 13 |
| Examinations | Weeks 14–16 |
| Result processing | 4–6 weeks after exams |
| Next semester begins | After short break |

_Exact dates vary each session — check ePortal and departmental notice boards_

## Exam Timetable

### How to Get Your Timetable
1. Log in to eportal.unilag.edu.ng
2. Navigate to **Examinations → Exam Timetable**
3. Filter by your faculty, department, and level
4. Download and print — keep a physical copy

### General Exam Rules
- Arrive **30 minutes before** exam start time
- Bring your **student ID** and **exam docket** (printed from portal)
- No phones in the exam hall — violation = exam cancellation
- Blue or black pen only
- Calculators: only approved scientific calculators (non-programmable) for science courses
- Extra answer booklets available from invigilators — raise your hand
- Dressing: No hats/caps in exam halls [VERIFY per faculty]

### Common Exam Periods
- **First Semester exams:** Usually January – February
- **Second Semester exams:** Usually June – July
- **Make-up/Special exams:** End of session, by special request [VERIFY process]

## Exam Docket
- Generated from the student portal after course registration and fee payment
- **Must be printed** — digital copy not accepted in most faculties [VERIFY per faculty]
- If you can't print, go to the nearest cybercafé in Akoka
- Lost docket: Go to your departmental exam officer with your course registration form

## Checking Results

### Method 1: Student Portal
1. Login to eportal.unilag.edu.ng
2. Navigate to **Results → Semester Results**
3. Results are uploaded faculty by faculty — not all at once

### Method 2: Departmental Notice Boards
- Some departments still post results physically
- Check both portal AND notice board

### Result Timeline
- Results typically released 4–8 weeks after exams
- Supplementary/carry-over results may take longer
- GPA/CGPA updates after all courses for that semester are graded

## Carry-Overs & Supplementary Exams

- **Carry-over:** Score below passing grade (F or E depending on department) → must retake
- **Supplementary exam:** Offered for some courses — check with your department
- **Maximum carry-overs:** Varies by faculty — too many can lead to withdrawal [VERIFY per faculty]
- **Spillover students:** Students who exceed normal programme duration — special rules apply

## Exam Malpractice
UNILAG takes this **very seriously**:
- Caught with unauthorized materials = immediate expulsion from exam
- Case referred to Senate Committee on Examination Malpractice
- Consequences: Rustication, suspension, or expulsion depending on severity
- Don't risk it — the consequences follow you for life

## Missing an Exam / Medical Absence
1. Inform your department **before or immediately after** the exam
2. Provide a medical certificate from UNILAG Health Centre (external hospital letters may not be accepted)
3. Apply for a special exam through HOD → Dean → Senate
4. This is a formal process — start it quickly

## FAQs

**Q: Results aren't on the portal — are they out?**
A: Results are released course by course, lecturer by lecturer. One missing result doesn't mean others are delayed. Check your departmental notice board too.

**Q: My result is wrong on the portal. What do I do?**
A: Report to your departmental exam officer immediately with evidence (your answer booklet if kept, your score sheet). They escalate to the lecturer for correction.

**Q: I scored an E — is that a pass?**
A: In most departments yes (1.0 grade point), but some departments require minimum C for core courses. Check your faculty handbook.

**Q: When is the supplementary exam?**
A: Usually at the end of the session. Check the academic calendar on the portal or ask your HOD.

**Q: Can I defer an exam?**
A: Not typically possible casually. Medical/compassionate grounds only, with proper documentation.
